White Space &
Clean Design

  • Mazda

    • mazdausa.com

    Mazda's menu are well-organized and easy to sift through. It's sophiscated and clean. Their website has a good use of white-space. The spacing around all the objects allows viewers to easily find what they are looking for.

    Clean design focuses on the careful and precise position of the important elements throughout the website. Mazda's website illustrates clean design very well.

    Rule of
    Thirds

  • Zara

    • zara.com

    The photo successfully uses the rule of thirds because it creates a feel of natural balance and perfect symmetry. The objects in the photo (the rock, the girl, and clothing) and the placement of the objects creates a feel and movement to it.

    The rule of thirds is used in web design to create balance within the images or other elements of the landing page. Zara's website followed the rule of thirds and well organized it through their design.

    PARC:
    Contrast

  • Apple

    • apple.com

    The bold rainbow of colors of the apple phones and black background of the apple watch are a huge contrast. Both objects and color choice are a powerful glow emanating from the screen.

    Contrast is the relationship between two or more design elements whose dramatic differences are emphasized when both elements are shown together. Apple's web design does a great job with contrast and bring both elements together.
